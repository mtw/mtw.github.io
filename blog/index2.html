Dec 16 2021 Current deep learning approaches cannot predict RNA secondary structures

Machine learning of RNA structure is more challenging than you might think. Using synthetic data from ViennaRNA's RNAfold to study the capabilities and shortcomings of neural networks for RNA secondary structure prediction in a controlled setting, we argue that shortcomings in the artificial setting will translate to real data

Posted by Michael T. Wolfinger on Thu 16 December 2021. updated Sun 09 October 2022

Sep 02 2021 Functional RNA Structures in the 3'UTR of Mosquito-Borne Flaviviruses

The 3'UTR of Mosquito-Borne Flaviviruses (MBFVs) contain evolutionarily conserved functional elements, such as exoribonuclease-resistant RNAs (xrRNAs), dumbbell elements or terminal stem-loops. Here we describe, annotate, and compare these elements within different serological groups of MBFVs.

Posted by Michael T. Wolfinger on Thu 02 September 2021. updated Wed 28 September 2022

May 15 2021 Secondary and tertiary structure of the BVDV Internal Ribosome Entry Site

Here we characterize a pseudoknot in the 5'UTR Internal Ribosome Entry Site (IRES) of Bovine Viral Diarrhea Virus (BVDV) using SHAPE-guided RNA structure probing and computational modelling of secondary and tertiary structures

Posted by Michael T. Wolfinger on Sat 15 May 2021. updated Sun 09 October 2022

Jul 26 2018 Searching for Telomerase RNAs in Saccharomycetes is TERribly Difficult

Telomerase RNAs are intrinsically difficult to find by homology search methods, even within phylogenetically related groups. This study reports our efforts in annotating telomerase RNAs in the Saccharomycetacea subgroup of yeasts, using the ViennaRNA Package and related tools

Posted by Michael T. Wolfinger on Thu 26 July 2018. updated Tue 27 September 2022

Jul 01 2018 Modeling co-transcriptional riboswitch folding under kinetic control

Using the 2'dG riboswitch as an example this study explains how co-transcriptional folding under kinetic control can be efficiently modeled with ViennaRNA tools
